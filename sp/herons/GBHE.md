Tall. Big. Blue. Birders want them, fish fear them.

<hr class="thin-line">

**Appearance:** tall, long-legged wading bird. Back and wings are mostly slate-grey, with darker remiges and median coverts; a rusty brown is visible on the shoulder, hand, and wrist. Has distinct plumes sprouting from its head and neck: dark slate-coloured crest on the head, and white plumes on the neck and upper chest. Rest of head is white, fading to brown from the neck down. Bill is long, sharp, and yellow. 

**Voice:** haven't heard anything from these guys yet :/

**Behaviour:** wades in search of smaller vertebrates, slowly stalking the water. Movements are painfully slow. Flies with head folded above neck.

**Known whereabouts:** wetlands

**First seen in:** St. Albert
