This woodpecker is named for both its fuzzy appearance and how it would fall down from the air if it had no limbs.

<hr class="thin-line">

**Appearance:** small-ish, fuzzy woodpecker. Mostly black and white, with three black stripes on the head and white-spotted wings. Males have a red patch of feathers near the back of their heads. Can be distinguished from the similar Hairy Woodpecker by size (smaller) and bill length (proportionately shorter).

**Voice:** "pik!", drumming is fast and constant.

**Behaviour:** climbs trees by latching onto trunks vertically, poking at crevices and chipping away at bark to access insects. 

**Known whereabouts:** deciduous trees

**First seen in:** Edmonton
